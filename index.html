My name is Andrew Cortez and I am a 22 year old Mathematics major at University of California, Riverside.

Mathematics has always been a passion of mine since I could remember. Being a senior in college has not allowed to have much professional experience, but I have learned alot in the four years of being at UCR. Being a Mexican American student at UCR I decided to join the Society of Hispanic Proffesional Engineers(SHPE) organization, where I learned a tremendous amount of knowledge and also gained alot of experience there. While in SHPE I joined the treasure committee where I would create a year long budget for the entire organization and monitored all the money that was being spent and how it was being spent. I also would reach out to local businesses and partner up with them so we could recieve sponsorship funds while we advertised the company.

I started my undergraduate career as an electrical engineering major, which was extremely benifical to me and introducted me to alot of peers that have helped me along the way. Even though I no longer an an engineering student, that experience that I recieved from being one is something that still helps me very much in the mathematics field. I am very glad I made the transition to mathematics as I realized I enjoyed it alot more. I am very passionate about what I study and even though my academic career is going to be ending soon, I still wish to continue learning wherever I decide to find a job, since there is never enough learning one can do.
